The coke oven is the core piece of equipment in the coking process.
The coal charging car is responsible for charging the blended coal into the coke oven.
The coke pusher pushes the finished coke out of the coke oven.
The coke quenching car transports the red-hot coke to the quenching tower.
Wet quenching cools red-hot coke with water.
Dry coke quenching cools the coke using an inert gas.
The coke screening building grades the coke by particle size.
The coal blending process determines the quality of the coke.
Heat-recovery coke ovens use waste heat to generate electricity.
Coke oven gas is an important byproduct.
The dust collection system ensures compliance with environmental requirements.
Production scheduling coordinates the operation of each process step.
The quality inspection center monitors product quality.
The electrical system provides power for the entire plant.
The instrumentation and automatic control system enables production automation.
Safety management is the top priority in production.
Equipment maintenance extends the service life of equipment.
The shift handover system ensures continuity of production.
The fire protection system safeguards plant safety.
Performance assessment verifies the design specifications.
