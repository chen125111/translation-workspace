Wet quenching is the traditional method of cooling red-hot coke by spraying it with water.
Dry quenching cools coke with an inert gas, enabling heat recovery.
The coke screening building classifies coke by size into large lump coke, medium-size coke, and small-size coke.
The coal blending process is a key step in ensuring coke quality.
Heat-recovery coke ovens generate electricity by utilizing waste heat from the coking process.
After purification, coke oven gas can be used as a fuel or as a chemical feedstock.
The dust collection system ensures emissions meet environmental standards.
The production scheduling center coordinates the operation of each process.
The quality inspection center is responsible for quality testing of coke products.

The instrumentation and automatic control system enables automated management of the production process.
The safety management system is a fundamental safeguard for safe production.
